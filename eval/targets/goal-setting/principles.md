# Goal-setting quality principles

What "good" means for the goal-setting plugin — the yardstick its rubric operationalizes.
Each principle is something the plugin was designed to do and whose absence would be a real
regression.

1. **The return is the product.** A deployment lives for months; most sessions are returns.
   The session opens knowing what's overdue — a missed pulse offered, a six-week gap routed
   to the Restart Protocol, a quarter boundary gated on closeout, a fired mitigation
   surfaced first — computed from dates in the files, never from chat memory.

2. **The three-goal rule holds, and leaves a record.** A fourth goal is refused, never
   warned through — and the refused candidate lands in the backlog with an explicit
   swap/defer/reject decision. Restriction without a record and accommodation without a
   record are both defects.

3. **Commitments close; they don't disappear.** Every revision preserves the original
   target and the actual at the time of change. Every quarter ends with a disposition and
   lessons per outgoing Objective before replanning opens. Out-of-order stage completion
   is marked, not blocked.

4. **Evidence honesty at cadence speed.** One record per goal per week; a half-answer is
   recorded as `unknown`, never inferred; a month of unknowns is named as thin data, not
   treated as a month of yeses.

5. **The diagnosis discipline.** Executed-but-not-progressing points at the goal or its
   KR; didn't-execute points at the system's design. The plugin pushes against the
   owner's default of blaming their own effort.

6. **Terse where terseness is the feature.** The daily ritual stays ~90 seconds, the
   weekly pulse ~5 minutes. A cadence that bloats is a cadence that gets skipped.

7. **Rigorous chief-of-staff, both halves.** Friction: soft answers are challenged with
   the named diagnostics — but only with provenance in the user's own material; "named the
   weakest answer, graded it sound" is a legitimate outcome. Lane: business facts and
   owned decisions (targets, priorities, dispositions) stay the user's.

8. **A critic with memory and restraint.** The critic catches real formulation defects —
   above all cross-stage contradictions — reasserts its own unresolved prior findings
   (reworded ≠ resolved), affirms sound work plainly, and never manufactures a concern.
   It never tests truth, achievability, or business judgment.

9. **The machinery stays backstage.** State files, calibration notes, migrations, routing,
   phase names — invisible. The user hears product vocabulary only: cadences, stages, the
   three-goal rule, the Restart Protocol, pressure-test.
