# Move

*Translate the strategy into action. Strategy that changes nothing was a thought experiment.*

Move is the seventh stage of the Strategy Spine, and it runs on Innovating: this is the bridge from a decided direction to a different reality. For where Move sits in the spine and why, see the [Strategy Spine overview](../README.md). This page covers what the stage produces, how to choose among its tools, and how it runs.

## What it produces

Owned, prioritised, sequenced actions and the conditions for execution. Recorded in `strategy/brief.md` under Move.

The stage is done when:

- **Every workstream has a named owner and a sequence,** not just a list of tasks.
- **The plan is real to the people who will run it,** not only to the strategist. The work of Move is making the plan executable, not making the plan.
- **The conditions for execution are mapped** — the decisions, communications, and dependencies that have to be in place — not assumed.

## Choosing the tool

Move holds 6 frameworks in two groups: tools that plan the execution, and tools that take it to market.

### Plan the execution — who does what, in what order

- [Execution Plan](execution-plan.md) — the integrated roadmap of goals, activities, leads, and milestones across workstreams. The backbone; the single source of truth for who does what, by when.
- [Capability Drop](capability-drop.md) — an outcome-based roadmap that answers "what will users be able to do, and when?" rather than "what tasks are being worked on?"
- [Decision Grid](decision-grid.md) — maps the key decisions each workstream faces against the weeks of the programme, so a steering group knows what to decide and when.
- [Zero To One](zero-to-one.md) — the five-phase launch sequence (Mobilise, Design, Build, Launch, Scale) for standing up something the team has never done before.

### Take it to market — land it with the audience

- [GTM Stack](gtm-stack.md) — maps every go-to-market component and the dependencies between product, channels, and backend, so nothing is missed.
- [Comms Deploy](comms-deploy.md) — a staged communications plan, so the right people hear the right thing at the right moment across the rollout.

Start from the Execution Plan as the backbone; the others specialise it. Capability Drop reframes it around outcomes, Decision Grid around governance, and GTM Stack and Comms Deploy around the launch.

## How it runs

The `strategist-stage` skill runs this stage. Working from the Story, it:

1. Translates the strategy into owned, sequenced workstreams using the planning tools above.
2. Maps the conditions for execution — decisions, communications, dependencies — so the plan can actually run.
3. Records the execution plan in `strategy/brief.md` under Move. This is a high-stakes stage where `/strategist:pressure-test` is offered.
4. Advances `strategy/STATE.md` to complete.

The loop closes here rather than ending. Move surfaces what no plan could predict — the market shifts, an assumption breaks, execution teaches what analysis couldn't. Adapting is the return arrow: you re-enter Define with a sharper question than the one you started with. A strategy is a position you hold and keep correcting, not a document you finish.
