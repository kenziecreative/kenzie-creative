# Analyse

*Interrogate the data behind each driver.*

18 frameworks.

- [Trend](trend.md) — A chart that traces how a single value (or two values side by side) moves across time — so you can see the direction, pace, and inflection points of change.
- [Comparison](comparison.md) — A chart that places discrete values side by side so the differences between them are immediately readable — the default tool for answering "which is bigger and by how much?"
- [Mix](mix.md) — A chart that shows how the proportional composition of a whole shifts between two points in time or across categories — so you can see not just totals, but who is winning share within them.
- [Distribution](distribution.md) — A chart that shows how a set of values spreads across a range — revealing whether the data clusters tightly in the middle, skews to one end, or splits into distinct groups.
- [Scatter](scatter.md) — A chart that plots individual data points across two axes to reveal whether — and how strongly — two variables relate to each other.
- [Cumulative](cumulative.md) — A chart that shows both the individual size of each category and the running total they build toward — making it immediately visible that a small number of categories accounts for most of the result.
- [Candlestick](candlestick.md) — A chart that shows the spread of a data set — minimum, maximum, and middle percentiles — as a single visual shape per category, so you can compare not just typical values but the range and variability of each.
- [Rank](rank.md) — A chart that tracks the changing position of multiple items across a series of time periods — so you can see not just who is winning, but who is gaining and who is losing ground.
- [Unit Economics](unit-economics.md) — A framework that examines the ongoing profit generated by a single customer over their lifetime — and compares it to the cost of acquiring them — to determine whether the business model works at the unit level before scaling.
- [Waterfall](waterfall.md) — A chart that explains the gap between a starting number and an ending number by showing every step that moved it — so you see not just *what* changed, but *what caused* the change.
- [Marginal Return](marginal-return.md) — A curve that shows how each additional unit of input — money, time, headcount — yields progressively less output, and eventually none at all.
- [Cashflow](cashflow.md) — A method of valuing a business or opportunity by mapping cash in and cash out over time, then discounting future flows back to today's value to arrive at a net present value.
- [ROIC](roic.md) — A metric that measures how efficiently a business converts its invested capital into operating profit — and a decomposition tree that shows exactly which lever is driving that efficiency.
- [Actual v Target](actual-v-target.md) — A chart that overlays what actually happened against what was planned — period by period — so variances are immediate and the direction of drift is clear at a glance.
- [Profit Margin](profit-margin.md) — A stacked bar chart that shows revenue, cost, and profit together in each period, with a margin percentage overlay — so you see not just how much profit was made, but how much of every revenue dollar survived as profit.
- [5 Why's](5-whys.md) — A root-cause technique that finds the underlying cause of a problem by asking "why" repeatedly — each answer becoming the input to the next question — until the chain bottoms out at something actionable.
- [5W+H](5w-h.md) — A six-angle investigation framework — Who, What, When, Where, Why, How — that maps the full terrain of a problem before you attempt to solve it.
- [Yes/No](yes-no.md) — A binary logic tree that guides a user to an answer by routing them through a sequence of yes-or-no questions, each branch leading either to the next question or to a defined outcome.
