# Story

*Make the strategy felt, not just understood. A strategy nobody can feel is a strategy nobody will carry.*

Story is the sixth stage of the Strategy Spine, and it runs on Feeling: narrative is how the work moves from understood to acted on. For where Story sits in the spine and why, see the [Strategy Spine overview](../README.md). This page covers what the stage produces, how to choose among its tools, and how it runs.

## What it produces

The narrative artifact — the document, deck, or page that carries the whole strategy in a form a person can absorb and repeat. Recorded in `strategy/brief.md` under Story.

The stage is done when:

- **The argument is structured answer-first;** the audience always knows where they stand.
- **The groupings are MECE:** nothing double-counted, nothing important left out.
- **The strategy is in a transmissible form and it's felt** — not just a logical case, but one a person will carry into rooms you aren't in.

## Choosing the tool

Story holds 7 frameworks in two groups: tools that structure the argument so the logic holds, and tools that shape the narrative so it lands. Structure first, then shape.

### Structure the argument — make the logic hold

- [Minto Pyramid](minto-pyramid.md) — organise the whole argument answer-first: answer, then reasons, then evidence.
- [HV Logic](hv-logic.md) — test that the pyramid actually holds, vertically and horizontally, before you present it.
- [MECE](mece.md) — test any list of reasons or groupings: mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive. A test you apply, not a narrative on its own.
- [SCQA](scqa.md) — open the communication with the problem (Situation, Complication, Question) before the Answer, so the audience feels they demanded the conclusion.

### Shape the narrative — make it land

- [Story Spine](story-spine.md) — the six-sentence arc from stable world to disruption to resolution, the shape that makes any story compelling.
- [5 ACT](5-act.md) — a forward-looking pitch arc: Trends, Challenges, Big Idea, Benefit, Differentiators.
- [StoryBrand](storybrand.md) — cast the customer as the hero and the brand as the guide; best for brand and marketing messages.

Build the structure first (Minto Pyramid, tested with HV Logic and MECE), then choose a narrative shape to deliver it. A beautiful story over a broken argument collapses under the first hard question.

## How it runs

The `strategist-stage` skill runs this stage. Working from the committed synthesis, it:

1. Structures the argument answer-first and tests that it holds, using the structure tools above.
2. Shapes it into a narrative form that lands, using the narrative tools above.
3. Records the narrative artifact in `strategy/brief.md` under Story.
4. Advances `strategy/STATE.md` to Move once the strategy is in a transmissible, tested form.

The loop runs backward when it has to. If telling the story exposes a gap the argument can't fill, that is a signal the synthesis isn't done — the stage points you back rather than papering the gap with rhetoric.
