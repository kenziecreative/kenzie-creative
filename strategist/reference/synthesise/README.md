# Synthesise

*Build the insights into one coherent whole, then commit. This is where the strategy becomes a strategy.*

Synthesise is the fifth stage of the Strategy Spine, and it runs on Creating: a coherent strategy is something new, built from parts that did not cohere on their own. For where Synthesise sits in the spine and why, see the [Strategy Spine overview](../README.md). This page covers what the stage produces, how to choose among its tools, and how it runs.

The decision is not a separate step after the strategy; it is the act of committing to the through-line you build here. That commitment locks at a gate before Story.

## What it produces

One coherent strategy with an explicit through-line, and a committed decision. Recorded in `strategy/brief.md` under Synthesise.

The stage is done when:

- **The insights are reconciled into a single argument,** not a list. Tensions are resolved, not averaged away.
- **Priorities are explicit:** what matters most is named, and what was set aside is named too.
- **The commitment gate is passed:** the decision is locked, with the deliberation matched to the stakes, before any narrative is built. Story does not begin until it is.

## Choosing the tool

Synthesise holds 8 frameworks in two groups: tools that help you build and prioritise the through-line, and tools for the commitment gate that locks the call.

### Prioritise and weight — what matters most?

- [Eisenhower](eisenhower.md) — sort the pieces by urgency and importance; see what to do, delegate, schedule, or drop.
- [ABCD](abcd.md) — match the rigor of each decision to its scope and impact versus your familiarity with it.
- [Evaluation](evaluation.md) — score every option against the same weighted criteria, turning a qualitative comparison into a number you can defend.
- [Bull & Bear](bull-and-bear.md) — weight a bet by its optimistic, base, and pessimistic scenarios, so you know what you're wagering on.
- [Decision Tree](decision-tree.md) — when choices have probabilistic outcomes, map them and compute the expected value with the math visible.

### The commitment gate — lock the call before Story

- [Bezos](bezos.md) — first ask how reversible the decision is. Type II (reversible) decisions deserve speed; Type I (irreversible) deserve the full weight of the gate.
- [Pros & Cons](pros-and-cons.md) — name the trade-offs of the chosen path explicitly, so a tangled choice becomes one you can actually make and stand behind.
- [SPADE](spade.md) — when the commitment is high-stakes and collaborative, run the structured commit: setting, people, alternatives, a private-vote decision, and an explanation everyone can align to.

The two groups run in order: build and prioritise the through-line first, then take it through the gate. Committing before you build is a preference in disguise; building without committing leaves Story nothing solid to carry.

## How it runs

The `strategist-stage` skill runs this stage. Working from the insights Insight produced, it:

1. Reconciles the insights into one coherent argument, resolving the tensions between them and naming the through-line.
2. Prioritises — names what matters most and what is being set aside — using the build tools above.
3. Runs the commitment gate: makes sure the through-line stands against real alternatives, then locks the decision with deliberation matched to its stakes, using the gate tools. The gate runs the reasoning critic automatically before the decision locks — never blocking; a declined check or an unresolved finding is recorded rather than argued, and it travels with the strategy.
4. Records the synthesis and the committed decision in `strategy/brief.md` under Synthesise.
5. Advances `strategy/STATE.md` to Story once the through-line is coherent and the gate is passed.

The loop runs backward when it has to. Synthesis is where contradictions surface: if reconciling the insights proves the analysis or the frame was wrong, the stage points you back rather than forcing a coherent-looking strategy on top of a broken foundation.
