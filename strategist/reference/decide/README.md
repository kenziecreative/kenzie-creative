# Decide

*Weigh the options and commit to a path.*

8 frameworks.

- [Eisenhower](eisenhower.md) — A 2×2 matrix that sorts every task by urgency and importance so you can see at a glance what to do yourself, what to delegate, what to schedule, and what to drop.
- [ABCD](abcd.md) — A four-category system that sorts decisions by their scope and impact versus your familiarity with them — so you can match the right level of process and involvement to each decision.
- [Decision Tree](decision-tree.md) — A branching diagram that maps your choices and their possible outcomes — with probabilities and payoffs attached — so you can calculate the expected value of each option and decide with the math visible.
- [Pros & Cons](pros-and-cons.md) — A structured side-by-side comparison of the positives and negatives of each option — with the trade-offs named explicitly — so a decision that feels tangled becomes a choice you can actually make.
- [Evaluation](evaluation.md) — A scoring matrix that applies the same criteria to every option and produces a weighted total — turning a qualitative comparison into a number you can defend, and a recommendation you can explain.
- [Bezos](bezos.md) — A two-category split — irreversible Type I decisions versus reversible Type II decisions — that tells you how much process and deliberation any given decision actually deserves.
- [SPADE](spade.md) — A five-step framework for making high-stakes decisions collaboratively — defining the setting, assembling the right people, generating real alternatives, deciding through private vote, and explaining the outcome so everyone commits.
- [Bull & Bear](bull-and-bear.md) — A three-scenario chart — optimistic Bull, neutral Base, and pessimistic Bear — that makes the upside and downside of a decision visible so decision-makers know exactly what they're betting on.
