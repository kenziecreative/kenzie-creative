# Post-Processing Styles Reference

This file defines color grading and post-processing aesthetics for Nano Banana Pro simulation. This is the "final grade" applied after lighting and camera simulation.

---

## Understanding Post-Processing

Post-processing affects:
- **Color Temperature:** Warm vs cool overall tone
- **Contrast:** Difference between lights and darks
- **Saturation:** Intensity of colors
- **Tone Curve:** How shadows, midtones, and highlights render
- **Color Grading:** Specific color shifts in shadows/highlights

---

## Commercial & Clean Styles

### Clean Commercial
- **Description:** True-to-life colors, high clarity, professional polish
- **Contrast:** Medium-high
- **Saturation:** Accurate, slightly enhanced
- **Colors:** Neutral, true-to-life
- **Shadows:** Clean, detailed
- **Highlights:** Bright, controlled
- **Best For:** E-commerce, product photography, corporate
- **Use When:** Need accurate representation; commercial work; professional output

### Editorial Commercial
- **Description:** Slightly stylized clean look with punch
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** Enhanced but natural
- **Colors:** Punchy, vivid
- **Shadows:** Deep but detailed
- **Highlights:** Bright, slightly clipped for impact
- **Best For:** Magazine ads, editorial products, lifestyle commercial
- **Use When:** Want commercial but with more visual impact

### High-Key Clean
- **Description:** Bright, airy, minimal shadows
- **Contrast:** Low
- **Saturation:** Reduced, pastel-like
- **Colors:** Light, bright, often slightly cool
- **Shadows:** Lifted, almost eliminated
- **Highlights:** Bright, pure whites
- **Best For:** Beauty, skincare, tech, minimalist brands
- **Use When:** Apple aesthetic; skincare; bright minimalist

### Studio Neutral
- **Description:** As shot, minimal processing, studio accurate
- **Contrast:** Natural
- **Saturation:** True to life
- **Colors:** Completely neutral
- **Shadows:** As captured
- **Highlights:** As captured
- **Best For:** Product accuracy, color-critical work
- **Use When:** Color accuracy is critical; client needs true representation

## Film-Inspired Styles

### Kodak Portra 400
- **Description:** Classic portrait film emulation, warm and creamy
- **Contrast:** Medium-low
- **Saturation:** Slightly muted, beautiful skin tones
- **Colors:** Warm highlights, slightly cyan shadows, peachy skin
- **Shadows:** Lifted, milky
- **Highlights:** Soft, warm rolloff
- **Best For:** Portraits, weddings, lifestyle
- **Use When:** Want that classic film portrait look; weddings; organic feel

### Kodak Portra 800
- **Description:** Higher contrast Portra with more grain feel
- **Contrast:** Medium
- **Saturation:** Slightly muted
- **Colors:** Similar to 400 but slightly cooler, more contrast
- **Shadows:** More defined than 400
- **Grain:** Simulated fine grain
- **Best For:** Indoor portraits, events, editorial
- **Use When:** Want Portra but with more punch

### Fuji Pro 400H
- **Description:** Clean, slightly cool, beautiful greens
- **Contrast:** Medium-low
- **Saturation:** Muted, elegant
- **Colors:** Cool highlights, beautiful greens, soft pastels
- **Shadows:** Clean, slightly lifted
- **Best For:** Weddings, fashion, outdoor portraits
- **Use When:** Want elegant, slightly cool film look; beautiful greens

### Kodak Ektar 100
- **Description:** Vivid, saturated, punchy film look
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** Very saturated
- **Colors:** Vivid, slightly warm, intense blues and greens
- **Shadows:** Deep, rich
- **Best For:** Landscape, travel, vibrant scenes
- **Use When:** Want maximum color impact; travel; landscapes

### Cinestill 800T
- **Description:** Tungsten-balanced cinema film, moody with halation
- **Contrast:** Medium
- **Saturation:** Muted with color shifts
- **Colors:** Teal shadows, warm highlights, red halation around lights
- **Special:** Halation glow around bright lights
- **Best For:** Night photography, urban, neon, moody scenes
- **Use When:** Night scenes; want that cinema film look; neon lights

### Ilford HP5 (B&W)
- **Description:** Classic black and white, versatile contrast
- **Contrast:** Medium-high
- **Grain:** Moderate
- **Tones:** Full range, classic look
- **Best For:** Street, portraits, documentary
- **Use When:** Want classic black and white; street photography

### Tri-X 400 (B&W)
- **Description:** Gritty, contrasty black and white
- **Contrast:** High
- **Grain:** Pronounced
- **Tones:** Deep blacks, bright whites
- **Best For:** Street, documentary, dramatic B&W
- **Use When:** Want gritty, high-contrast B&W; documentary style

## Cinematic Styles

### Cinematic Teal & Orange
- **Description:** Classic Hollywood blockbuster color grade
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** Stylized, complementary colors
- **Colors:** Orange skin tones, teal/cyan shadows and backgrounds
- **Shadows:** Deep teal
- **Highlights:** Warm orange
- **Best For:** Dramatic portraits, movie-poster look, action
- **Use When:** Want Hollywood blockbuster feel; dramatic impact

### Cinematic Desaturated
- **Description:** Muted, serious, prestige drama look
- **Contrast:** Medium-high
- **Saturation:** Significantly reduced
- **Colors:** Muted, serious, often slightly cool or brown
- **Shadows:** Deep, detailed
- **Best For:** Serious subjects, dramatic portraits, editorial
- **Use When:** Want serious, prestige drama aesthetic

### Film Noir
- **Description:** High contrast black and white with dramatic shadows
- **Contrast:** Very high
- **Colors:** Black and white
- **Shadows:** Deep black, lost detail intentional
- **Highlights:** Bright, harsh
- **Best For:** Dramatic B&W, mystery, artistic
- **Use When:** Maximum drama; artistic B&W; mystery mood

- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** High for neons, dark elsewhere
- **Colors:** Hot pink, electric blue, neon green against dark
- **Shadows:** Very deep, almost black
- **Best For:** Night urban, neon scenes, sci-fi aesthetic
- **Use When:** Cyberpunk aesthetic; neon-lit scenes

### Warm Vintage Cinema
- **Description:** 70s film look, warm, slightly faded
- **Contrast:** Medium-low
- **Saturation:** Muted, warm
- **Colors:** Warm overall, slightly faded, brown-orange tint

- **Highlights:** Warm, soft
- **Best For:** Nostalgic, retro, period pieces
- **Use When:** Want nostalgic feel; retro aesthetic

## Moody & Artistic Styles

### Moody Film
- **Description:** Deep shadows, desaturated, atmospheric
- **Contrast:** Medium
- **Saturation:** Significantly reduced, especially greens/blues
- **Colors:** Desaturated greens and blues, skin tones preserved
- **Shadows:** Deep, detailed but dark
- **Highlights:** Controlled, not too bright
- **Best For:** Dramatic portraits, editorial, fine art
- **Use When:** Want moody, atmospheric feel; editorial work

### Dark & Moody
- **Description:** Very dark, selective lighting, mysterious
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** Low
- **Colors:** Near monochromatic, deep tones
- **Shadows:** Very deep, lost detail
- **Highlights:** Selective, dramatic
- **Best For:** Fine art, dramatic products, atmospheric
- **Use When:** Maximum moodiness; dark artistic look

### Matte Film
- **Description:** Lifted blacks, soft contrast, dreamy
- **Contrast:** Low
- **Saturation:** Slightly muted
- **Colors:** Soft, pastel-like
- **Shadows:** Heavily lifted, no true blacks
- **Highlights:** Soft
- **Best For:** Lifestyle, weddings, soft editorial
- **Use When:** Want dreamy, soft, romantic feel

### Faded Retro
- **Description:** Faded colors, lifted shadows, vintage feel
- **Contrast:** Low
- **Saturation:** Significantly reduced
- **Colors:** Faded, nostalgic
- **Shadows:** Very lifted
- **Highlights:** Soft, slightly yellow
- **Best For:** Nostalgia, retro aesthetic, artistic
- **Use When:** Want aged, vintage, nostalgic look

## Specialty Styles

### HDR Natural
- **Description:** Extended dynamic range but natural-looking
- **Contrast:** Compressed (detail in shadows AND highlights)
- **Saturation:** Slightly enhanced
- **Colors:** Natural but vivid
- **Shadows:** Lifted, detailed
- **Highlights:** Controlled, detailed
- **Best For:** Real estate, interiors, challenging lighting
- **Use When:** Need detail in both bright windows and dark corners

### Cross-Processed
- **Description:** Vintage darkroom technique, color shifts
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** Variable
- **Colors:** Unexpected color shifts (cyan highlights, yellow shadows)
- **Best For:** Artistic, retro, experimental
- **Use When:** Want unexpected color shifts; artistic experimentation

### Infrared
- **Description:** Simulated infrared film look
- **Contrast:** High
- **Saturation:** N/A (typically B&W or false color)
- **Colors:** White foliage, dark skies, dreamy
- **Best For:** Landscape, artistic, surreal
- **Use When:** Want surreal, dreamy landscapes

- "High Fashion contrast with Matte Film blacks"
