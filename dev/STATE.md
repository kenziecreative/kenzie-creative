## Session knowledge worth keeping

- **The read-by-nobody family has a third form, and it is the one that hides:** not *config read by no step*, and not *schema field written by nobody*, but **a field written by a step and read by no step.** It is perfectly implemented on the write side, so it survives every check that asks "is this wired up?" — and it is pure decoration. Two shipped in v1.0.0 (`last_status`, the run windows) and survived STOP B's targeted sweep of the other two forms. **The sweep to run is: for every field in a schema, grep for a *consumer*; if the only hits are the definition, the writer, and the docs, the behavior it was supposed to enforce is not happening.** This caught four of my own write-only fields in v1.1.0 before they shipped. It should become a mechanical lint across the marketplace.
- **New family, and it generalizes past this plugin: an obligation without a recorded outcome is theater.** Any step a skill declares *mandatory* must leave a durable trace of whether it succeeded, or every downstream claim that depends on it ("nothing surfaced," "collection current," "coverage complete") is unfalsifiable. This is pass-1's captured-contract-read-by-gate one level up: there a captured *value* was read by no gate; here a mandatory *operation* was recorded by no field.
- **Codex's diagnoses are worth more than its prescriptions.** Confirmed again: 9/9 citations verified, 0 refuted across two passes — but its fixes consistently cost more than the defects require (this time: re-open and re-extract every emitted claim, on a product that was already facing a 100+ search first run). **Take the diagnosis; re-price the remedy.** It is epistemically strong and product-cost blind, and it does not know these are solo-operator conversational tools.
- **Disclosing the locks in the pass-2 prompt worked exactly as intended.** Told the verifier subagent was locked, Codex wrote *"This does not require a verifier subagent"* and routed around it. Zero lock collisions this pass, versus several in pass 1 — which is itself evidence the locks are drawn in the right places. **Keep disclosing them.**
- **Two findings can multiply into a third that neither reviewer sees.** Codex found the no-stagger defect and the zero-due status contradiction as separate items and never combined them. Together they meant a daily brief would produce *nothing at all* six mornings out of seven. **When triaging a finding list, cross the findings against each other before pricing them** — the interaction was more dangerous than any single item on the list.
- **The one-shot build held, but the post-build sweep still earned its keep:** four real gaps survived a spec-faithful build, all in the family pass-1 taught (config read by no step; schema field written by nobody). **On any future one-shot build, run that family sweep before tagging** — it found what the ten-point checklist could not.
