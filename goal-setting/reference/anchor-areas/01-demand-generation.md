# 1. Demand Generation

*Anchor area chapter — companion to the [Goal Setting Playbook](../playbook.md). For the body-systems metaphor, the scorecard mechanics, and how to choose where to focus, see the [Anchor Areas README](README.md).*

> **In the body:** Demand Generation brings in the oxygen.

A lighthouse doesn't run around the coast looking for boats to save. It stands in one place and shines. Effective demand generation isn't about chasing customers; it's about becoming a beacon that attracts the right people to you.

**The Belief:** If I build a great product, customers will find me.

**The Reality:** The best product doesn't win; the best-known product does. Demand generation is the work of getting noticed by the right people in a crowded world. It's not just marketing; it's the entire process of creating awareness and attracting people who have the problem you solve.

**The Practice:** Stop thinking about marketing as a separate activity and start thinking about it as a core function of your business. Identify where your ideal customers gather and create content that is genuinely useful to them. Build partnerships with other businesses that serve the same audience. The goal is not to be everywhere, but to be in the right places, consistently.

**What It Means:** You shift from a "build it and they will come" mindset to a "go to them and bring them back" mindset. You understand that your product is only as valuable as the number of people who know it exists. You treat marketing not as an expense, but as an investment in your future growth.

**Signals of Strength:**

- You have a steady stream of inbound leads from multiple channels.
- Your customers can clearly articulate what you do and why it matters.
- You are seen as a trusted voice in your industry.

**Common Failure Modes:**

- Relying on a single channel for all your leads.
- Confusing activity (posting on social media) with results (generating qualified leads).
- Trying to appeal to everyone and ending up appealing to no one.
