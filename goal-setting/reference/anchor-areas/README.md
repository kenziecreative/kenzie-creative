# The Seven Anchor Areas

*The companion reference to the Goal Setting Playbook.*

---

## What this document is

The Goal Setting Playbook uses Anchor Areas as a structural element — they're how you decide where to focus, where to set goals, and where to build systems. The playbook names the seven and gives you the scorecard mechanics. This companion gives you the depth: what each area actually is, why it matters, how it commonly fails, and what good looks like.

Use the playbook for the methodology. Use these chapters when you need to understand what an anchor area means before you score yourself in it, or when your scorecard surfaces an area as weak and you want to know what to do about it.

Each area has its own file:

1. [Demand Generation](01-demand-generation.md) — getting noticed by the right people.
2. [Conversion](02-conversion.md) — turning interest into action.
3. [Delivery](03-delivery.md) — creating genuine customer value.
4. [Operations](04-operations.md) — the systems that let the team work together.
5. [Nurturing](05-nurturing.md) — the ongoing work of customer relationships.
6. [Profit](06-profit.md) — the financial sustainability that funds everything else.
7. [Personal Leadership](07-personal-leadership.md) — the leader who runs it all.

## How the seven fit together

Six of the seven are functional areas of the business. The seventh is the leader who runs it.

A business is a living organism, and each anchor area is a vital organ. You can't have a healthy heart without healthy lungs. The body-systems metaphor:

- **Demand Generation** brings in the oxygen.
- **Conversion** turns it into blood.
- **Delivery** is the circulatory system that carries it to the muscles.
- **Operations** is the nervous system that coordinates everything.
- **Nurturing** is the immune system that keeps the body healthy.
- **Profit** is the food that provides the energy for it all.
- **Personal Leadership** is the consciousness directing the body — without it, the other systems run, but they can't adapt, learn, or choose.

A weakness in any one area puts stress on the entire system. Too much focus on Demand Generation without a solid Conversion process is like inhaling without being able to absorb oxygen. Great Delivery with poor Nurturing is like having strong muscles you never use. The goal is not to be perfect in all seven, but to be aware of how they interact and to focus your energy on the one currently holding the entire system back.

The seven aren't a checklist to complete. They're a system to balance.

## Using the scorecard

The Anchor Areas Scorecard is the diagnostic tool that connects these chapters to the playbook. Rate yourself honestly on a 1-10 scale in each area:

- **1-3:** Completely reactive, no systems.
- **4-6:** Inconsistent, happens when you remember.
- **7-8:** Pretty good, occasional slip-ups.
- **9-10:** Rock solid, automatic.

A few notes on how to use it well:

**Don't average — look at the spread.** A business with all 6s is in a very different position than one with three 9s and four 3s. The spread tells you what kind of business you're running. Tight clusters of mediocrity hide problems; wide spreads make priorities obvious.

**Score what is, not what should be.** The most common mistake is scoring what you wish were true. Use the signals of strength as a check — if you can't honestly claim them, you're not at that level yet.

**Re-score at every quarterly review.** Scores move. A 4 in Operations becomes a 7 after a quarter of focused system-building. A 7 in Delivery decays to a 5 after two key hires leave. The quarterly re-score is what keeps the scorecard alive instead of historical.

**Personal Leadership decays silently.** Of all seven areas, this is the one most likely to drop without you noticing. Schedule it for an extra honest look — and consider having someone outside the business help you score it.

**The weakest area isn't always where to start.** Three legitimate selection logics:

- *Weakest score* — fix the vulnerability.
- *Most urgent* — even if it's not the weakest, if a cash crunch is six weeks out, Profit wins.
- *Highest leverage* — sometimes a 7 in Conversion is your highest-leverage move because every other improvement compounds through it.

Pick by whichever logic actually fits your situation. But pick few — the playbook's max-three rule applies here too. Three active anchor areas is the ceiling. One is often the right answer.

## How this connects to the playbook

The playbook references these chapters at Stage 3 (Anchor Areas). When using the playbook:

- Read the relevant chapter before scoring yourself in an area you don't fully understand.
- After scoring, read the chapter for your weakest (or most urgent, or highest-leverage) area to inform what kind of Objective and systems would actually move it.
- During the quarterly review, return to the chapters for any anchor area whose score moved meaningfully — the signals of strength and common failure modes will sharpen your read on what changed and why.

The playbook is the method. These chapters are the map of the territory the method operates on.
