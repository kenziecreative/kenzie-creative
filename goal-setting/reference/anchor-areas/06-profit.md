# 6. Profit

*Anchor area chapter — companion to the [Goal Setting Playbook](../playbook.md). For the body-systems metaphor, the scorecard mechanics, and how to choose where to focus, see the [Anchor Areas README](README.md).*

> **In the body:** Profit is the food that provides the energy for it all.

A tree needs strong roots to grow tall. Profit is the root system of your business. It's not the goal, but it's the essential fuel that allows you to pursue your goal. Without a sustainable financial model, even the most noble mission will eventually fail.

**The Belief:** If I focus on growth, profit will follow.

**The Reality:** Growth without profitability is a recipe for disaster. Profit is about more than just making money; it's about building a sustainable business. It's about understanding your unit economics, managing your cash flow, and making strategic decisions about where to invest your resources. It's about ensuring that your business is not just growing, but growing stronger.

**The Practice:** Develop a clear understanding of your financial model. Optimize your pricing strategies to capture the full value of your product. Explore new revenue streams that align with your core mission. Your goal is not to maximize short-term profit, but to build a business that can thrive for the long term.

**What It Means:** You stop thinking about profit as a dirty word and start seeing it as a tool for achieving your mission. You understand that financial sustainability is not at odds with your purpose; it is essential to it. You build a business that is not just successful, but also resilient.

**Signals of Strength:**

- You have a clear and predictable path to profitability.
- You are able to fund your growth without relying on external investment.
- You are making strategic decisions that are in the long-term best interest of your business.

**Common Failure Modes:**

- Chasing revenue growth at all costs.
- Not understanding the true cost of acquiring a customer.
- Failing to adapt your business model as the market changes.
