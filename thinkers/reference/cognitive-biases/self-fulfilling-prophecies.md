# Self-Fulfilling Prophecies

> A prediction that causes itself to become true, due to the behavior it generates.

## Description

A Self-Fulfilling Prophecy is a belief that creates its own reality. You believe something will happen, and that belief changes your behavior in a way that makes it happen. It's a feedback loop between expectation and outcome. Think of a bank run. If enough people believe a bank is going to fail, they will all rush to withdraw their money. This mass withdrawal will cause the bank to fail. The belief created the reality it predicted.

## How It Works

This works psychologically because our expectations influence our behavior, which in turn affects the outcome, reinforcing the original belief in a cyclical manner.

## Signature Phrasing

- "belief creates reality"
- "expectation changes behavior"
- "feedback loop between belief and outcome"
- "prophecy fulfills itself"

## Ask Yourself

> Is there evidence that this outcome would occur independently of my expectations or actions?

## Applying This Knowledge

### Everyday

If you believe you're going to fail a job interview, you'll be more nervous, less confident, and less prepared. This will make you more likely to fail. Your belief has become a self-fulfilling prophecy.

*Be mindful of the stories you tell yourself. Your beliefs are not just passive predictions; they are active forces that shape your behavior and your outcomes. Choose your beliefs wisely.*

### At Work

If a manager believes a new hire is going to be a poor performer, they might give them less challenging work, less feedback, and less support. As a result, the new hire underperforms. The manager's belief has created the outcome they expected.

*Challenge your initial judgments about people. Give everyone the benefit of the doubt and the opportunity to succeed. Your expectations are a powerful force; use them to lift people up, not to hold them down.*

### In Education

If a teacher believes a student is gifted, they will treat that student differently—with more attention, more encouragement, and higher expectations. The student will rise to meet those expectations. This is the Pygmalion Effect, and it's a powerful example of a self-fulfilling prophecy.

*Believe in your own potential. Surround yourself with people who believe in you. The expectations of others can become a self-fulfilling prophecy, so choose your environment carefully.*
