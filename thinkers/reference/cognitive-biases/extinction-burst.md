# Extinction Burst

> The temporary increase in a behavior that is being targeted for extinction.

## Description

An Extinction Burst is the last, desperate gasp of a dying habit. When you try to stop reinforcing a behavior, the behavior will often get worse before it gets better. The system is making one last, frantic attempt to get the reward it is used to receiving. Think of a child who is used to getting a candy bar every time they throw a tantrum in the grocery store. The first time the parent decides to stop giving in, the child doesn’t just stop crying; they throw the biggest, loudest, most epic tantrum of their life. This is the extinction burst. The behavior is escalating in a final, desperate attempt to get the old reward.

## How It Works

The behavior temporarily intensifies because the system expects the usual reinforcement and escalates its efforts to obtain the reward before extinction occurs.

## Signature Phrasing

- "Behavior gets worse before it gets better"
- "Last desperate attempt"
- "Escalating behavior"
- "Trying one last time for reward"

## Ask Yourself

> Is this increase in behavior a sign of true persistence or a temporary escalation before it stops?

## Applying This Knowledge

### Everyday

You decide to stop checking your phone every five minutes. For the first hour, the urge to check it is almost unbearable. You feel more anxious and more distracted than you did before. This is the extinction burst of your phone-checking habit.

*The key to surviving an extinction burst is to know that it is coming, and to not give in. If you can ride out the storm, the behavior will eventually start to fade. If you give in, you have just taught the system that the way to get the reward is to escalate the behavior.*

### At Work

A company decides to stop rewarding managers for hoarding resources and to start rewarding them for collaborating. In the short term, the resource-hoarding behavior might actually get worse, as the managers make one last, desperate attempt to protect their turf in the old system.

*When you are trying to change a culture, expect an extinction burst. The old culture will fight back. You need to have the resolve to stay the course and to continue reinforcing the new behaviors, even when the old ones are screaming for attention.*

### In Education

You are trying to break a bad habit, like procrastinating on a big project. You start working on the project for an hour every morning. On the third day, you have an overwhelming urge to just skip it and watch Netflix instead. This is the extinction burst of your procrastination habit.

*Use the “ten-minute rule.” Tell yourself you only have to work on the project for ten minutes. After ten minutes, you can stop if you want to. More often than not, the act of starting is enough to get you over the hump of the extinction burst.*
