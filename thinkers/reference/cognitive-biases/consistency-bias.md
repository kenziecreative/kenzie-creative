# Consistency Bias

> We incorrectly remember our past attitudes and behavior as resembling our present attitudes and behavior.

## Description

Consistency bias is the tendency to remember past beliefs, attitudes, values, and behavior as more consistent with your current self than they really were.

The mind smooths the personal timeline. Old uncertainty, contradiction, growth, and reversal become less visible, making the current identity feel like it was always there.

## How It Works

A coherent self-story is psychologically useful. It reduces dissonance and makes life feel continuous.

But when coherence overwrites accuracy, people lose access to how they actually changed, what they once misunderstood, and what evidence moved them.

## Recognition Cues

- You remember having "always" believed what you believe now.
- Old records surprise you.
- Your past self seems unusually consistent, rational, or aligned.
- You underremember doubts, mistakes, or changes of mind.
- You are impatient with people who are where you used to be.

## Signature Phrasing

- "Reconstructing past to fit present"
- "Editing memories for coherence"
- "Claiming consistent past beliefs"
- "Creating smooth personal narratives"
- "I've always been this way"

## Ask Yourself

> Can I find evidence that contradicts my current beliefs about my past?

## Common False Positives

Some values and traits really are stable. Recognizing continuity is not automatically bias.

The bias appears when the desire for continuity erases evidence of real change, contradiction, or learning.

## What Helps

- Keep decision journals, notes, and dated commitments.
- Review old artifacts before telling the story of what happened.
- Treat past inconsistency as evidence of learning, not shame.
- Ask what you used to believe and why it made sense then.
- Use records in retrospectives rather than memory alone.

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: supported in autobiographical memory and attitude-change research; effects vary with identity relevance and availability of records.
