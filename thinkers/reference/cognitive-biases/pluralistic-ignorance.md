# Pluralistic Ignorance

> A situation where a majority of group members privately reject a norm, but assume (incorrectly) that most others accept it.

## Description

Pluralistic ignorance happens when many people privately disagree with a norm, belief, or behavior but incorrectly assume that most others accept it.

Because everyone reads everyone else's silence as agreement, the norm persists even when private support is weak.

## How It Works

People use public behavior to infer private belief. But public behavior is shaped by fear, politeness, hierarchy, uncertainty, and desire to belong.

When everyone is performing acceptance for social safety, each performance becomes evidence to others that acceptance is genuine. The group creates a false consensus from mutual misreading.

## Recognition Cues

- People privately complain but publicly agree.
- Meetings end in silence even though side conversations are full of doubt.
- Everyone waits for someone else to say the obvious thing.
- Newcomers quickly learn "how we act here" without anyone defending the norm.
- People are surprised to discover how many others shared their concern.

## Signature Phrasing

- "Everyone else seems to agree"
- "I must be the only one who thinks this"
- "No one is speaking up"
- "It must be fine if no one disagrees"
- "I do not want to be the difficult one"

## Ask Yourself

> What private disagreement might be hidden behind public silence?

## Common False Positives

Sometimes silence really is agreement, fatigue, lack of concern, or trust in the process.

The bias is most likely when there are social costs to dissent, ambiguous norms, power differences, or private signs that public agreement is thinner than it looks.

## What Helps

- Create low-risk ways to surface private views: anonymous polls, private pre-reads, silent writing, or round-robin input.
- Ask, "What might someone disagree with but not want to say?"
- Separate dissent from disloyalty.
- Have leaders state uncertainty first so others have permission to be honest.
- Treat silence as missing data, not consent.

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: well-established as a social norm phenomenon, especially where public behavior is a poor signal of private belief.
