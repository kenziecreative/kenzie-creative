# Introspection Illusion

> The tendency for people to overestimate their ability to understand their own mental states, and to believe that their self-assessments are more reliable than their assessments of others.

## Description

The introspection illusion is the tendency to overestimate how well you understand your own motives, preferences, biases, and mental processes.

It does not mean introspection is useless. It means inner experience is not a complete audit trail. You can often report what you feel and believe, but you may be less reliable about why you feel or believe it.

## How It Works

The mind creates coherent explanations after the fact. Those explanations can be sincere, detailed, and wrong.

Because your own reasons are experienced from the inside, they feel more trustworthy than other people's self-reports. This creates an asymmetry: "I know my true reasons, but they are just rationalizing."

## Recognition Cues

- Your motives feel obvious and transparent to you.
- You treat your explanation of your behavior as stronger evidence than the behavior itself.
- You dismiss external feedback because "I know what I meant."
- You assume bias is easier to see in others than in yourself.
- You can explain a choice fluently, but the explanation changes when challenged.

## Signature Phrasing

- "I know why I did that."
- "I am sure that was not bias."
- "I would know if that affected me."
- "My motives are clear to me."
- "They are rationalizing, but I am explaining."

## Ask Yourself

> What would my behavior suggest if I could not rely on my own explanation?

## Common False Positives

People do have meaningful access to their internal states. Feelings, intentions, values, and memories matter.

The bias appears when introspection is treated as decisive proof, especially against behavioral evidence, outside feedback, or known influence conditions.

## What Helps

- Compare your explanation with your behavior over time.
- Ask what a neutral observer would infer from the same actions.
- Use structured decision processes where bias is likely.
- Treat feedback as data about impact, even if it does not match intent.
- Say "my current explanation is..." rather than "the reason is..."

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: well-supported as a limitation of self-knowledge and causal introspection, though people vary in self-awareness by domain and feedback quality.
