# Learned Helplessness

> A state of passivity and inaction that results from repeated exposure to uncontrollable negative events.

## Description

Learned helplessness is a state of passivity that can develop after repeated exposure to uncontrollable negative outcomes.

The person learns that effort does not matter. Later, even when conditions change and action could help, the expectation of futility suppresses trying.

## How It Works

Repeated uncontrollable setbacks teach a prediction: "Nothing I do changes the outcome." That prediction can become generalized beyond the original situation.

Over time, the person may stop scanning for options. Inaction then produces more bad outcomes, which can seem to confirm the belief.

## Recognition Cues

- "Why bother?" appears before options are examined.
- The person does not try even when low-risk actions exist.
- Past failure is treated as proof that future effort is pointless.
- Help, options, or changed conditions are dismissed automatically.
- Agency feels emotionally unavailable, not just intellectually unclear.

## Signature Phrasing

- "Stop trying"
- "Nothing I do matters"
- "It's out of my control"
- "I can't escape"
- "It never changes"

## Ask Yourself

> What evidence do I have that my actions can change the outcome now?

## Common False Positives

Some situations really are constrained. Calling realistic constraint "learned helplessness" can become victim-blaming.

The key question is whether there are now meaningful levers of control that the person no longer perceives or tests.

## What Helps

- Identify one small, real lever of control.
- Design actions with visible feedback.
- Separate old environments from current conditions.
- Rebuild agency through small wins, not motivational speeches.
- Avoid blaming the person for adaptations learned under constraint.

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: well-established as a concept from animal and human learning research, with modern interpretations emphasizing control, attribution, depression, and environment.

## Related Patterns

- [The Illusion of Control](./the-illusion-of-control.md) — Learned helplessness underestimates control; illusion of control overestimates it.
- [Just-World Bias](./just-world-bias.md) — Outsiders may blame helplessness on character while ignoring uncontrollable conditions.
- [Self-Handicapping](./self-handicapping.md) — Both can protect against painful evaluation, but self-handicapping preserves excuse-making while learned helplessness suppresses effort.
- [Procrastination](./procrastination.md) — Avoidance can become habitual when effort feels futile.
- [Status Quo Bias](./status-quo-bias.md) — Staying with a painful familiar state can feel safer than testing agency.
