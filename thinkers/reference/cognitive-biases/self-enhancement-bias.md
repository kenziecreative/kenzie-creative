# Self-Enhancement Bias

> The tendency to view oneself in an overly favorable light.

## Description

Self-enhancement bias is the tendency to see yourself in an overly favorable light.

It is broader than self-serving bias. Self-serving bias explains outcomes in a flattering way. Self-enhancement bias shapes the underlying self-image: more competent, ethical, objective, generous, intelligent, fair, or self-aware than the evidence warrants.

## How It Works

Positive self-belief can support confidence and resilience. But the mind often protects that self-belief by discounting weaknesses, inflating strengths, and selecting flattering comparisons.

Because self-image is partly invisible from the inside, the bias often feels like simple accuracy: "I am just being honest about my strengths."

## Recognition Cues

- You rate yourself above average on traits where most people cannot all be above average.
- Feedback about weaknesses feels less credible than praise.
- You assume your motives are cleaner than other people's motives.
- You compare yourself to weaker reference groups when evaluating ability.
- You describe your flaws in ways that still sound flattering.

## Signature Phrasing

- "I am more objective than most people."
- "I am not really biased."
- "My flaw is that I care too much."
- "People do not appreciate how much I bring."
- "I am above average at this."

## Ask Yourself

> What evidence would make me lower my estimate of myself in this area?

## Common False Positives

Healthy confidence is not self-enhancement bias. Some people accurately assess their strengths, and underconfidence can be its own problem.

The warning sign is insulation from correction: when self-image remains flattering no matter what feedback, outcomes, or comparisons show.

## What Helps

- Use specific behavioral evidence instead of global self-ratings.
- Ask for examples, not impressions: "When did I do this well or poorly?"
- Compare self-ratings with peer, customer, or outcome data.
- Notice which feedback you immediately disqualify.
- Keep confidence tied to practice, evidence, and learning, not identity.

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: well-supported as a broad self-perception tendency, though size and direction vary by culture, trait, domain, and measurement method.
