# Priming

> Prior exposure to a cue making related thoughts, feelings, interpretations, or responses more accessible later.

## Description

Priming is a cueing mechanism. Something you encounter first can make related ideas easier to notice or use afterward. A word, image, question, smell, setting, story, or recent experience can tilt what feels salient in the next moment.

The practical point is not that people are puppets. It is that attention has a recent history. What has just been activated can shape what comes next, especially when people are moving quickly, interpreting ambiguity, or relying on intuition.

## How It Works

Mental concepts are connected. Activating one concept can temporarily increase access to nearby concepts. If a meeting begins with risk, failure, and blame, later ideas may be interpreted through caution. If it begins with possibility, users, and prototypes, the same room may think differently.

Some priming effects are small, short-lived, and context-dependent. Strong claims require caution.

## Recognition Cues

- A recent cue seems to color later interpretation.
- The first question in a conversation shapes the answers that follow.
- A setting makes certain behavior feel more natural.
- Repetition makes a phrase or option feel familiar.
- You notice yourself using language that was introduced earlier.

## Signature Phrasing

- "That put the idea in my head."
- "Now I cannot unsee it."
- "The first example shaped the whole conversation."
- "We started in a fear mindset."
- "That cue changed the frame."

## Ask Yourself

> What was made salient before this judgment, and would I think differently if the setup had been different?

## Common False Positives

Priming is one of the most overextended ideas in popular psychology. Some famous social priming claims have replication problems or much smaller effects than originally implied.

Do not use priming as a universal explanation for behavior. Use it as one possible contributor, especially when cues are repeated, meaningful, and close to the decision.

## What Helps

Be deliberate about openings. The first example, first metric, first story, or first question often sets the field. In important decisions, reset the room by naming multiple frames before choosing one.

For self-management, make wanted behaviors more visible and unwanted behaviors less cued. Environment design is usually more reliable than willpower alone.

## Evidence Status

Basic cognitive priming is well-supported. Broad behavioral priming claims are mixed and sometimes contested. The safest use is modest: cues can influence accessibility, but they do not determine behavior by themselves.
