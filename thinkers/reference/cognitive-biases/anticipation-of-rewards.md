# Anticipation of Rewards

> People are more excited by the prospect of a reward than the reward itself.

## Description

Anticipation of rewards is the tendency for the expectation of a reward to become highly motivating, sometimes more motivating than the reward itself.

The imagined future payoff can generate energy, desire, and persistence. It can also lead people to chase the feeling of wanting rather than the reality of having.

## How It Works

Reward systems respond strongly to cues, uncertainty, novelty, and near-misses. Wanting can intensify before receiving.

This helps people pursue goals, but it also makes variable rewards, countdowns, bonuses, shopping, gambling, notifications, and status signals unusually compelling.

## Recognition Cues

- Planning or chasing feels better than receiving.
- You quickly start wanting the next reward after getting the current one.
- Variable or uncertain rewards pull more attention than fixed rewards.
- The imagined payoff is richer than the actual experience.
- You confuse wanting with liking.

## Signature Phrasing

- "The journey is the destination"
- "It's the thrill of the chase"
- "I can't wait for..."
- "The best part is the anticipation"
- "Just one more"

## Ask Yourself

> Am I pursuing the reward itself, or the feeling of almost having it?

## Common False Positives

Anticipation can be healthy. Looking forward to something can increase motivation, meaning, and enjoyment.

The bias becomes costly when anticipation drives compulsive loops, unrealistic expectations, or neglect of whether the reward actually satisfies.

## What Helps

- Compare wanting before the reward with satisfaction after it.
- Design rewards that connect to real values, not only cues.
- Watch variable-reward loops that keep attention hooked.
- Add pauses before escalating pursuit.
- Ask what happens after the reward is obtained.

## Evidence Status

Evidence status: supported by reward-learning and motivation research; details depend on cue design, uncertainty, individual differences, and context.
