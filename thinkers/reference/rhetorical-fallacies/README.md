# Rhetorical Fallacies

6 entries.

- [Appeal to Emotion](appeal-to-emotion.md) — Manipulating an emotional response in place of a valid or compelling argument.
- [Argument by repetition](argument-by-repetition.md) — Repeating a claim persistently to make it seem true despite lack of evidence or logic.
- [Motte-and-bailey](motte-and-bailey.md) — A rhetorical tactic where a controversial claim is defended by retreating to a much easier, uncontroversial one when challenged.
- [Moving the goalposts](moving-the-goalposts.md) — Changing criteria for success or agreement after they have been met to avoid conceding a point.
- [Poisoning the well](poisoning-the-well.md) — Discrediting an opponent before they speak to bias the audience against them.
- [Proof by assertion](proof-by-assertion.md) — Repeating a statement as true without evidence to force acceptance despite lack of proof.
