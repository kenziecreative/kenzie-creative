# The Long-Term Vision

> A clear picture of a desired future that helps people choose, persist, and coordinate over time.

## Description

A long-term vision describes the future you are trying to make real. It is not a full plan. It is a direction-setting image that helps people understand what their effort is for and what tradeoffs are worth making.

The best visions are concrete enough to guide decisions and open enough to survive learning. They create alignment without pretending the path is already known.

## Mechanism

Vision works by organizing attention and sacrifice. If people can see the future state, they can choose between competing opportunities, tolerate delay, and coordinate without constant instruction.

A vision also creates identity: "We are the kind of people building toward this." That can be motivating, but it can also make correction harder if the vision becomes sacred.

## When to Use

Use a long-term vision when work is complex, slow, uncertain, or distributed across many people. It is useful in leadership, personal planning, product strategy, community building, and long projects.

It matters most when short-term discomfort would otherwise make people abandon the larger purpose.

## When It Backfires

Vision backfires when it becomes fantasy without strategy. A vivid future with no credible path can become false hope.

It also backfires when it becomes too rigid. If the world changes, a vision should guide adaptation, not prevent it.

## Practical Use

- Describe the future state in terms people can recognize.
- Name why it matters and what tradeoffs it implies.
- Pair the vision with near-term evidence and next actions.
- Revisit the vision when new information changes what is possible.

## Decision Prompt

What future are we trying to make real, and what should that future cause us to choose differently today?
