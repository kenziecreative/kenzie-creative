# Authority

> The influence created when people treat a person, role, credential, or institution as a legitimate guide.

## Description

Authority helps people coordinate under complexity. When expertise, responsibility, and legitimacy are real, authority lets groups act without re-litigating every decision from scratch.

The danger is deference without judgment. People may obey titles, uniforms, credentials, confidence, or institutional status even when the authority is outside its competence or acting unethically.

## Mechanism

Authority works by reducing uncertainty and shifting responsibility. If someone appears qualified or empowered, others may assume they know more, see more, or carry the right to decide.

It can be earned through competence and trust, granted through role, borrowed through symbols, or faked through performance.

## When to Use

Use authority ethically by making real expertise, responsibility, and decision rights visible. Good authority clarifies who is accountable, why a decision is credible, and where challenge is still welcome.

As a reader of behavior, use this lens when people stop evaluating the argument and start deferring to the person or title behind it.

## When It Backfires

Authority backfires when it suppresses dissent, hides error, or makes people outsource moral responsibility. It also fails when authority claims exceed actual competence.

A leader who demands obedience faster than they earn trust may get compliance while losing truth.

## Ethical Boundary

Ethical authority increases clarity and responsibility. Manipulative authority uses status cues to bypass evidence, consent, or conscience.

The ethical question is whether authority is helping people make a better decision or making them afraid to question a bad one.

## Practical Examples

- A doctor explains the basis of a recommendation rather than relying only on status.
- A manager clarifies which decision is theirs to make and which parts are open to challenge.
- A speaker uses impressive credentials to push claims outside their expertise; the audience should separate the person from the evidence.

## Decision Prompt

Am I trusting the claim because the authority is relevant and accountable, or because the authority cue made me stop thinking?
