# Empathy Mapping

> A collaborative tool used to gain a deeper insight into customers and other stakeholders.

## Description

An Empathy Map is a simple but powerful tool for getting out of your own head and into someone else’s. It’s a quadrant where you map out what a person is Thinking, Feeling, Seeing, and Hearing in a particular situation. The goal is to move beyond a superficial understanding of someone and to develop a richer, more empathetic picture of their experience.

## When to Use

Use this whenever you are designing a product, a service, or a communication for a specific audience. It’s a great tool for design thinking, user research, and marketing. It’s also a powerful tool for improving your communication and collaboration with your own team members.

## When It Backfires

It can become a box-ticking exercise if it’s not done with genuine curiosity. If you are just filling out the quadrants with your own assumptions, you are not building empathy; you are just reinforcing your own biases. The map is only as good as the real-world observation and listening that goes into it.

## Practical Examples

- A product team is designing a new banking app for senior citizens. They create an empathy map for a typical user, thinking about their anxieties around technology (Feeling), the small print they can’t read on their phone (Seeing), the advice they are getting from their children (Hearing), and their desire for security and simplicity (Thinking).
