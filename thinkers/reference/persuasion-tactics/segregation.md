# Segregation

> Separating one part of the information from the wider context so it receives disproportionate attention.

## Description

Segregation persuades by isolating a detail, cost, benefit, metric, example, or category from the broader picture. The audience is encouraged to focus on the separated piece as if it were the decision.

This can help clarify, but it can also distort. What gets separated becomes salient; what remains outside the frame becomes easier to ignore.

## Mechanism

Attention is limited. When a speaker says "focus on this," they shape what the audience treats as relevant.

Segregation is closely related to framing and choice bracketing. It changes the boundaries around the decision.

## Where It Shows Up

- Marketing that highlights one attractive feature while isolating total cost.
- Policy debates that focus on one dramatic case while ignoring base rates.
- Workplace dashboards that separate one metric from downstream effects.
- Personal decisions where one benefit is considered apart from long-term cost.

## When It Persuades Legitimately

It is legitimate when isolating a variable helps people understand it, especially in complex systems.

Good segregation returns the isolated detail to context before a final decision is made.

## When It Becomes Manipulative

It becomes manipulative when the separated detail is used to prevent full-context evaluation.

If the audience is discouraged from asking "compared to what?" or "what else changes?" the focus has become distortion.

## Recognition Cues

- One metric is treated as the whole story.
- Context is described as distraction.
- The isolated detail is emotionally vivid.
- Reintegrating the detail into the full picture changes the conclusion.

## How to Respond

Ask what context has been excluded, what system the detail belongs to, and what tradeoffs appear when the whole picture is restored.

Evaluate the separated item both alone and in context.

## Decision Prompt

What has been separated from the full picture, and how does the judgment change when it is put back?
