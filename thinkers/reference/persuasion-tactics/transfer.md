# Transfer

> Moving feelings from one symbol, person, group, or idea onto another target.

## Description

Transfer persuades by association. A proposal, product, person, or policy is paired with something people already admire, fear, trust, resent, or identify with. The emotion attached to the source then colors the target.

It works without proving the target deserves the borrowed feeling.

## Mechanism

The mind uses associations as shortcuts. Symbols, music, imagery, respected figures, national identity, professional identity, family language, or past victories can make a claim feel warmer, safer, nobler, or more threatening.

The persuasion happens through emotional carryover rather than direct evidence.

## Where It Shows Up

- Ads pairing products with admired lifestyles or identities.
- Political messages wrapped in national or moral symbols.
- Workplace proposals tied to founding stories or past wins.
- Debates that associate an opponent with a disliked group or image.

## When It Persuades Legitimately

Transfer is legitimate when the association is relevant and truthful. Values, history, and symbols can help people understand why something matters.

For example, connecting a policy to a genuinely held organizational value can clarify priorities.

## When It Becomes Manipulative

It becomes manipulative when the association substitutes for evidence. A symbol of trust does not make a claim true. A disliked association does not make an opponent wrong.

Transfer is especially risky when it borrows moral authority without earning it.

## Recognition Cues

- Strong imagery or symbolism appears where evidence should be.
- Approval or disgust arrives before the claim is explained.
- The association is emotionally powerful but logically thin.
- Criticism feels like disrespecting the symbol rather than evaluating the proposal.

## How to Respond

Separate the source feeling from the target claim. Ask what evidence supports the target if the symbol, celebrity, story, or group identity is removed.

Name the association without mocking the value it invokes.

## Decision Prompt

What feeling is being transferred, and does the target actually deserve it?
