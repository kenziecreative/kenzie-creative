# Fear-uncertainty-doubt (FUD)

> Raising fear, uncertainty, or doubt to make people hesitate, reject, or delay without proportionate evidence.

## Description

FUD persuades by making an option feel unsafe, unknown, or irresponsible. It does not need to disprove the option. It only needs to make people uncomfortable enough to stay still, choose the familiar, or distrust the alternative.

The tactic often works through insinuation: "Are we really sure?" "What if something goes wrong?" "There are serious questions."

## Mechanism

People are loss-sensitive and uncertainty-averse. FUD activates caution while avoiding the burden of a full argument.

Because the risk is vague, it can be hard to answer. Clear evidence can rebut a claim; diffuse anxiety keeps moving.

## Where It Shows Up

- Incumbents warning against new competitors.
- Political campaigns emphasizing vague threats.
- Workplace resistance to change.
- Marketing that casts alternatives as risky or unproven.
- Debates that create doubt without engaging evidence.

## When It Persuades Legitimately

Risk warnings are legitimate when they are specific, evidenced, proportionate, and paired with a path for evaluation.

Good caution says what the risk is, how likely it is, what impact it would have, and how it can be tested or mitigated.

## When It Becomes Manipulative

It becomes manipulative when uncertainty is exaggerated to protect the status quo, discredit a rival, or stop inquiry.

The tactic is especially suspect when the speaker benefits from delay and refuses to define the risk clearly.

## Recognition Cues

- Vague warnings outnumber specific evidence.
- Worst-case scenarios are treated as likely.
- The current option is treated as safe without scrutiny.
- Questions create anxiety but no testable claim.

## How to Respond

Ask for the specific risk, likelihood, evidence, comparison point, and mitigation. Compare the risk of action with the risk of inaction.

Turn diffuse fear into a concrete risk register.

## Decision Prompt

Is this a real risk being clarified, or uncertainty being amplified to stop movement?
