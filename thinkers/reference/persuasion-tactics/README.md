# Persuasion Tactics

14 entries.

- [Appeal to rivalry](appeal-to-rivalry.md) — Motivating action by framing the situation as a contest against a rival person, group, or identity.
- [Aspirational framing](aspirational-framing.md) — Linking a choice to a desired future self, group identity, or better world.
- [Card stacking](card-stacking.md) — Presenting true but selective information so the audience sees only the side that supports your conclusion.
- [Contrast effect](contrast-effect.md) — Changing how an option feels by placing it next to a more extreme, inferior, or emotionally loaded comparison.
- [False concreteness](false-concreteness.md) — Making an abstract, uncertain, or speculative claim feel concrete without providing real grounding.
- [Fear-uncertainty-doubt (FUD)](fear-uncertainty-doubt-fud.md) — Raising fear, uncertainty, or doubt to make people hesitate, reject, or delay without proportionate evidence.
- [Future faking](future-faking.md) — Promising a desirable future to extract present trust, labor, loyalty, money, forgiveness, or compliance without a concrete path to deliver.
- [Glittering generalities](glittering-generalities.md) — Vague, positive, emotionally appealing language that invites approval without concrete support.
- [Identity Activation](identity-activation.md) — Making a particular identity salient so people feel pulled to act consistently with it.
- [Manufactured certainty](manufactured-certainty.md) — Presenting uncertainty as settled fact to reduce questioning and speed agreement.
- [Scapegoating](scapegoating.md) — Assigning broad blame to a target so a group can avoid complexity, responsibility, or uncertainty.
- [Segregation](segregation.md) — Separating one part of the information from the wider context so it receives disproportionate attention.
- [Strategic vagueness](strategic-vagueness.md) — Deliberately unclear language that creates favorable impressions while avoiding commitment.
- [Transfer](transfer.md) — Moving feelings from one symbol, person, group, or idea onto another target.
