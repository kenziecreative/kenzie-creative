# High-Risk Mislabels

Some of the most useful concepts in the corpus are also easy to misuse. This guide separates nearby patterns so the toolkit supports judgment rather than reflexive labeling.

## Gaslighting vs Disagreement vs Misremembering

Use [Gaslighting](../manipulation-tactics/gaslighting.md) when there is a repeated pattern of reality-undermining: denial, distortion, confidence, and pressure that makes the target doubt their own perception.

Use disagreement when two people interpret the same event differently and both can still engage the evidence. Use misremembering when the conflict is about memory limits rather than control. Memory really is fallible, and the [Misinformation Effect](../cognitive-biases/the-misinformation-effect.md) can distort recall without anyone deliberately manipulating.

| Question | Gaslighting | Disagreement or Misremembering |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern over time | Repeated reality reversal | Occasional conflict or inconsistency |
| Power move | One person's version must become reality | Both versions can be examined |
| Response to evidence | Evidence is dismissed, reframed, or punished | Evidence can update the conversation |
| Effect on target | Growing self-doubt and dependence | Frustration, but not loss of self-trust |

First move: keep a record, seek outside reference, and stop trying to win the manipulator's agreement about reality. If the other person is acting in good faith, a shared timeline or concrete evidence should help rather than escalate the distortion.

## Boundary Setting vs Control

Use [Boundary Setting](../strategies/boundary-setting.md) when someone is naming what they will do to protect their own time, body, attention, values, or participation.

Use manipulation concepts like [Isolation](../manipulation-tactics/isolation.md), [Information Control](../manipulation-tactics/information-control.md), or [Emotional Blackmail](../manipulation-tactics/emotional-blackmail.md) when someone is trying to control another person's relationships, information, choices, or emotional consequences.

| Question | Boundary | Control |
|---|---|---|
| Locus | "Here is what I will do." | "Here is what you must do." |
| Agency | Preserves both people's choice | Narrows the other person's choice |
| Transparency | Clear reason and clear limit | Shifting rule, guilt, fear, or dependence |
| Exit | The other person can disagree or leave | Disagreement is punished or made costly |

First move: translate the statement into "I will..." or "you must..." If it only works as "you must," inspect for control. If it can stand as "I will," it may be a boundary even if it disappoints someone.

## Persuasion vs Manipulation

Persuasion tries to change someone's mind while preserving their ability to evaluate. Manipulation tries to produce a response while bypassing, narrowing, or exploiting that ability.

Ethical persuasion can use emotion, story, framing, identity, and urgency. The question is whether those tools clarify the choice or obscure it. Compare [Aspirational Framing](../persuasion-tactics/aspirational-framing.md), [Transfer](../persuasion-tactics/transfer.md), [Manufactured Urgency](../manipulation-tactics/manufactured-urgency.md), and [Narrative Capture](../manipulation-tactics/narrative-capture.md).

| Question | Persuasion | Manipulation |
|---|---|---|
| Information | Gives enough to evaluate | Withholds, floods, distorts, or hides |
| Choice | The person can say no cleanly | Refusal is punished, rushed, or made shameful |
| Emotion | Emotion is relevant to the decision | Emotion is engineered to override judgment |
| Accountability | Claims can be checked | Influence works through implication or pressure |

First move: ask, "What would the clean version of this request be?" If the clean version is weak, hidden, or unacceptable, the influence may be doing work the argument cannot do.

## Appeal to Emotion vs Legitimate Emotional Relevance

Use [Appeal to Emotion](../rhetorical-fallacies/appeal-to-emotion.md) when emotion substitutes for evidence or relevance. Use emotional relevance when the feelings are part of the actual matter: harm, grief, fear, dignity, trust, belonging, safety, or meaning.

Manipulation entries like [Manufactured Fear](../manipulation-tactics/manufactured-fear.md), [Manufactured Pity](../manipulation-tactics/manufactured-pity.md), and [Emotional Blackmail](../manipulation-tactics/emotional-blackmail.md) are about engineered emotional states that constrain choice.

| Question | Legitimate Emotion | Appeal to Emotion |
|---|---|---|
| Relevance | Emotion is evidence of impact or value | Emotion distracts from the claim |
| Proportion | Intensity fits the stakes | Intensity is inflated to force agreement |
| Checkability | Facts and feelings can both be examined | Examining facts is framed as heartless |
| Choice | The listener can care and still disagree | Disagreement is treated as cruelty |

First move: separate the layers. "I accept that this matters emotionally. What factual claim or decision follows from that?" Good emotional reasoning survives that separation.
