# User Journey Guide

Use this guide when you are trying to apply the corpus to a real situation rather than study it entry by entry.

## 1. Name What Is Happening

Start with the behavior, not the label.

- What happened, in observable terms?
- What changed in attention, emotion, options, or accountability?
- Is this a one-time event or a repeated pattern?
- Who benefits if the current framing is accepted?

Then choose the likely pattern family:

- Bad argument or deflection: start with [Bad-Faith Moves](../bad-faith-moves/README.md), [Logical Fallacies](../logical-fallacies/README.md), or [Rhetorical Fallacies](../rhetorical-fallacies/README.md).
- Distorted judgment or self-check: start with [Cognitive Biases](../cognitive-biases/README.md).
- Influence or pressure: start with [Persuasion Tactics](../persuasion-tactics/README.md) or [Manipulation Tactics](../manipulation-tactics/README.md).
- Decision or interaction model: start with [Strategies](../strategies/README.md).

## 2. Check My Own Thinking

Before applying a label to someone else, ask what might be happening in you.

- Am I under stress, threat, shame, urgency, or fatigue?
- Am I explaining my own behavior with context and theirs with character?
- What evidence would change my mind?
- Am I using the label to understand the situation, or to stop understanding it?

Useful starting entries:

- [Actor-Observer Bias](../cognitive-biases/actor-observer-bias.md)
- [Confirmation Bias](../cognitive-biases/confirmation-bias.md)
- [The Affect Heuristic](../cognitive-biases/the-affect-heuristic.md)
- [Information Avoidance](../cognitive-biases/information-avoidance.md)
- [Loaded Language](../manipulation-tactics/loaded-language.md)

## 3. Respond to Manipulation

When a pattern looks manipulative, do not start by trying to prove the label. Start by protecting clarity.

- Return to the original question.
- Ask for the direct request.
- Slow down urgency.
- Get information from the source.
- Put agreements in writing.
- Seek outside reference.
- Stop arguing with reality distortion.

- [Emotional Blackmail](../manipulation-tactics/emotional-blackmail.md)

## 4. Make a Decision

When the goal is a better choice, separate the decision from the pressure around it.

- What is the decision, stated plainly?
- What are the options I am treating as unavailable?
- What information would I want if I had more time?
- What are the future costs, not just the past costs?
- What would I choose if I were advising a friend?

## 5. Practice Pattern Recognition

The goal is not to memorize names. The goal is to see mechanisms.

Practice with a recurring situation:

1. Write the situation in neutral language.
2. Name the pressure on attention, emotion, identity, evidence, or choice.
3. Pick two possible patterns, not one.
4. Look for false positives.
5. Choose one small response that increases clarity or agency.

Good practice sets:

- Influence: [Framing](../strategies/framing.md), [The Framing Effect](../cognitive-biases/the-framing-effect.md), [Loaded Language](../manipulation-tactics/loaded-language.md), [Transfer](../persuasion-tactics/transfer.md).
- Group dynamics: [Groupthink](../strategies/groupthink.md), [Pluralistic Ignorance](../cognitive-biases/pluralistic-ignorance.md), [Peer Pressure](../manipulation-tactics/peer-pressure.md), [The Bystander Effect](../cognitive-biases/the-bystander-effect.md).
- Overload: [Gish Gallop](../bad-faith-moves/gish-gallop.md), [Argument by Verbosity](../bad-faith-moves/argument-by-verbosity.md), [Flooding the Zone](../manipulation-tactics/flooding-the-zone.md).

The best sign that the toolkit is working: you become slower to accuse, faster to notice mechanisms, and clearer about what action would improve the situation.
