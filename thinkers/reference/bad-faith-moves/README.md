# Bad Faith Moves

8 entries.

- [Argument by verbosity](argument-by-verbosity.md) — Burying a weak or evasive point under excessive detail, jargon, tangents, or length.
- [Doublespeak](doublespeak.md) — Language designed to obscure, sanitize, or invert meaning so people cannot easily name what is happening.
- [Euphemism as cover](euphemism-as-cover.md) — Replacing accurate language with softer language to reduce moral weight or prevent an appropriate reaction.
- [Gish gallop](gish-gallop.md) — Overwhelming an opponent with many weak claims so careful rebuttal becomes impossible.
- [Kafka trap](kafka-trap.md) — Treating a person's denial of an accusation as further evidence that the accusation is true.
- [Sealioning](sealioning.md) — Repeatedly demanding evidence or explanation in bad faith while performing politeness.
- [Tone policing](tone-policing.md) — Deflecting from the substance of a message by focusing on the speaker's emotional delivery.
- [Whataboutism](whataboutism.md) — Deflecting accountability by pointing to another wrong instead of addressing the issue at hand.
